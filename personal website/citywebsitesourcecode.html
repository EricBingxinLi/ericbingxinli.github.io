		  <h2 class="section-heading">History</h2>
		  <div class="history-card">
			<p>Tokyo began as a small fishing village called Edo. It gained in prominence as it was the residence of Tokugawa Ieyasu, who became shogun of Japan in 1603.</p>
			<br>
			<p>In 1869, the emperor Meiji moved to Edo, which became the capital of Japan. Subsequently, the name was changed to Tokyo, meaning “eastern capital”.</p>
			<br>
			<p>Though mostly destroyed during World War II, Tokyo has grown back to become a major world metropolis.</p>
		  </div>

		  <article class="attraction-block attraction-toyosu">
			<h3 class="attraction-title">Toyosu fish market</h3>
			<p>Opening in 2018, the Toyosu Market replaced the historic Tsukiji fish market as the largest seafood market in the world.</p>
			<br>
			<p>In the daily seafood auctions, the highest quality fish are a hotly contested commodity. The best tuna have sold for over 1 million yen per kilogram (about $10,000).</p>
			<br>
			<p>You can view the auctions from one of the observation decks. Be sure afterwards to stop by the restaurants and shops to enjoy some fresh seafood.</p>

		  <article class="attraction-block attraction-sumo">
			<h3 class="attraction-title">Ryogoku Sumo Hall</h3>
			<p>Opening in 1985, the Ryogoku Sumo Hall is home to three of Japan’s six yearly honbasho, i.e. major sumo tournaments.</p>
			<br>
			<p>A honbasho lasts for 15 days, beginning and ending on a Sunday. Results in these tournaments will determine each wrestler’s promotion or demotion in the official sumo rankings.</p>

		  <!-- Shibuya -->
		  <article class="attraction-block attraction-shibuya">
			<h3 class="attraction-title">Shibuya</h3>
			<p>One of Tokyo’s major business and shopping centers, Shibuya is home to many department stores, as well as Shinjuku and Shibuya stations, the world’s two busiest train stations.</p>
			<br>
			<p>Shibuya is well known for the bustling scramble crossing in front of Shibuya Station’s Hachiko exit.</p>
			<br>
			<p>If you’re looking for something more peaceful, visit Yoyogi Park. Thousands of people visit Yoyogi Park each spring for cherry blossom viewing.</p>
